Project Overview

LinkWaveAI is an AI-powered platform designed for the construction industry, helping professionals access project information, understand regulations, and streamline communication across different stakeholders.

As a UX/UI Designer, I worked closely with the Product Owner to transform broad product requirements into a structured user experience. Due to the niche nature of the industry and limited access to end users, research relied heavily on desk research, workflow analysis, and continuous validation with domain experts.

The Challenge

Designing for a niche construction market with limited user access, scarce industry references, and broad initial requirements.

Our Approach

Applied Design Thinking to reduce uncertainty and validate assumptions throughout the process.

  • Conducted desk research and workflow analysis.
  • Collaborated closely with the Product Owner.
  • Refined the problem through iterative validation.
  • Designed user flows, wireframes, and prototypes.
  • Supported implementation through testing and feedback.

Design Insights

From Complexity to Clarity

Designing for construction professionals meant working with large amounts of technical information, documentation, and project-related discussions.

  • Structured Information Architecture — Organizing complex construction-related information into understandable flows.
  • Context-Aware AI Experience — Helping users interact with AI without losing track of project context.
  • Cross-Platform Consistency — HMaintaining a seamless experience across web and mobile environments.

Key Takeaway

Designing Beyond Assumptions

Working on LinkWaveAI reinforced the importance of validating assumptions when direct user access is limited.

The project strengthened my ability to navigate ambiguity, collaborate closely with domain experts, and use Design Thinking as a practical framework for solving unfamiliar and complex problems.
